My Story...

Well, to start things off,my name is Gwendolyn Stephenson and my family is European. I'm 2nd generation American. My grandparents moved to America in 1964 after fleeing from their countries, Czechoslavakia and Yugoslavia because of the communist regime that they were under after the second World War. I'm on a personal mission to obtain success in the field of Information Technology, I'm driven and hungry for more, and I'm going to make this huge world my oyster!

Partnership
for the
Future

Partnership for the Future is an organization with a dedicated network of local high schools, colleges and businesses in which they provide crucial resources for promising students with limited means who have their sight set on a college education - and an ambitious career. I was in 9th grade when I first heard of the program and decided to apply to get in, which I did. They helped prepare me for a lot of things I was looking towards... going to college, a promising career... PFF has also offered me an internship at
!!!Capital One !!!
for the summer going into my Junior year of Highschool. There was no way I could pass this opportunity up.

Internship
at
CapitalOne

In the summer of 2016, I had the opportunity to intern at Capital One and was paid!!! Going into it, I had no idea which department I was going into until the first day orientation. I was going to be working on the IT side of things and my particular "pod" was Enterprise Operations... Now to be honest I had no clue what exactly I was doing, but my manager, Iva MacFarland, lead me in the right direction and helped spark my interest in the tech field.
She taught me a lot on infrastructure, databases, what languages are used for such things, Iva made sure I learned a bit of SQL, HTML, CSS, and JS, and even informed me about Capital Ones switch over to the AWS cloud!
This was my first job ever while in public school, and I knew when I got older I wasn't going to settle for less.

Virginia
Commonwealth
University

After High School, I was excited to go to college, excited to go get a degree in something I loved, but I was torn between computer information systems/science or music education... A lot of my decisions were purely based off what others may have wanted for me. So I went to college, struggling financially for music education while at the same time in the marching band at Virginia Union University... I decided to leave VCU after one semester due to the fact I wasn't fortunate to pay out of pocket since financial aid thought my parents were making enough to help support me, but to be honest we were struggling big time and I had no choice but to leave. I loved music but I just felt so burnt out from highschool and I just didn't know if that was for me. SO after I left, I got a job and just worked while I tried to figure out what I was in to or at least try to find a different means of making my way in the world doing something I may enjoy. On the bright side, I left VCU with a 3.56 GPA so if I ever wanted to go back I could.
